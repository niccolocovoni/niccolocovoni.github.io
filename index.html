Niccolò Covoni

Researcher at Sophia University Institute · Philosophy of Physics · Philosophy of Information

I am a researcher at the Sophia University Institute and a PhD candidate at the University of Urbino Carlo Bo (Research Methods in Science and Technology program) and at the Institute of Philosophy (ISFI) of the Università della Svizzera italiana (USI) in Lugano.

My research lies at the intersection of Philosophy of Physics, Philosophy of Information, and Metaphysics of Science. I work on the conceptual and formal foundations of information in physics, with particular attention to entropy, quantum information, Relational Quantum Mechanics, and the ontological status of physical correlations.

My PhD project is supervised by Prof. Claudio Calosi and Prof. Vincenzo Fano. It investigates whether a physically significant notion of information can be articulated across thermodynamics, statistical mechanics, Shannon information theory, and quantum theory, focusing especially on Gibbs, Shannon, and von Neumann entropy.

I was a visiting student at the Rotman Institute of Philosophy at Western University as part of the QISS (Quantum Information Structure of Spacetime) project, under the supervision of Francesca Vidotto. I have also been a visiting student at the Faculty of Mathematics and Computer Science at the University of Łódź, under the supervision of Hanna Podsędkowska. More recently, I was an FTG Visiting Fellow at the Foundations of Thermodynamics Group at Nanyang Technological University (NTU), hosted by Eugene Y. S. Chua.

I hold a Bachelor’s Degree in Philosophy from the University of Pisa, with a thesis on the foundations of Quantum Logic, and a Master’s Degree in Logic, Philosophy and History of Science from the University of Florence, with a thesis on Quantum Information.

Research

My work investigates information as a physical and philosophical concept, with particular attention to entropy, quantum theory, and the relational structure of physical reality.

Philosophy of Information

Conceptual and ontological questions concerning information, entropy, correlations, and physical representation.

Quantum Foundations

Relational Quantum Mechanics, quantum information, measurement, probability, and Segal entropy.

Entropy and Thermodynamics

Foundational issues in thermodynamics, statistical mechanics, Shannon entropy, and von Neumann entropy.

Metaphysics of Science

Scientific realism, perspectivism, relational ontology, models, abstraction, and fundamentality.
